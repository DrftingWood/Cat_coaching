## 4. Core explanation (1:00 – 4:00)

### Idea 1: Percent is just "divide by 100"
> VO: The word "percent" means "per hundred". So twenty percent is literally twenty divided by one hundred, which is zero point two. Whenever you see a percent sign, replace it mentally with division by 100 and keep moving.

> ANIMATION: `FormulaBox` transforming `20\%` → `\tfrac{20}{100}` → `0.20`.

### Idea 2: "X percent of Y" is multiplication
> VO: "Twenty percent of one fifty" is just zero point two times one fifty. The word "of" is the multiplication sign wearing a disguise.

> ANIMATION: `FormulaBox` showing `20\% \text{ of } 150 = 0.20 \times 150 = 30`.

## 5. Worked example (4:00 – 5:30)
- Problem statement: A shopkeeper marks an item 40% above cost price and offers a 25% discount on the marked price. Find his profit percent.
- Step 1: Let cost price = 100.
- Step 2: Marked price = 100 + 40% of 100 = 140.
- Step 3: Selling price = 140 − 25% of 140 = 140 − 35 = 105.
- Step 4: Profit = 105 − 100 = 5, so profit percent = 5%.

> VO: Let's lock it in with a classic CAT-style problem...

> ANIMATION: `ProblemBox` with the stem, then `StepReveal` of the four steps.

## 6. Common traps (5:30 – 5:50)
> VO: Two traps. One: a 40% markup followed by a 25% discount is not a 15% profit — percentages don't add like that. Two: the discount is on the marked price, not the cost price.

## 7. Summary & CTA (5:50 – 6:00)
> VO: Two rules. Percent means divide by 100. "Of" means multiply. Nail these, and every percentage question becomes a decimal multiplication.
